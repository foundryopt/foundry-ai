# Change Order Workflow

## Purpose

Define a controlled process for initiating, evaluating, approving, and executing change orders — ensuring every scope, cost, and schedule change is documented, priced, and authorized before work proceeds.

## Scope

All active construction projects across all entities. Applies to project managers, site superintendents, design consultants, subcontractors, and procurement.

## Control: No Work on Unapproved Changes

**No work related to a proposed change may begin until the change order is fully executed (signed by all required parties).**

- Verbal approvals do not constitute authorization.
- If site conditions require immediate action before formal approval, the project manager must document a field directive in writing, obtain owner's representative sign-off, and flag the change order as "directed — pending formal CO" in the log.
- Any cost or schedule impact from a field directive is tracked against the change order.

## Procedure

1. **Identify change.** Any party identifies a scope, cost, or schedule deviation. Sources include: RFI responses requiring design changes, field conditions, owner-requested modifications, or code/regulatory changes.
2. **Submit potential change order (PCO).** Originator submits a PCO with: description, reason for change, affected drawings/specs, and preliminary cost/schedule impact estimate.
3. **Log PCO.** Project manager assigns a sequential PCO number and records it in the change order log with submission date.
4. **Evaluate impact.** GC solicits pricing from affected subcontractors. Design team confirms scope alignment and any required drawing revisions. Procurement flags material lead-time impacts.
5. **Decision gate: Approve, reject, or revise.**
   - Owner's representative reviews the fully priced PCO.
   - **Approve** — PCO converts to a formal change order (CO). Proceed to step 6.
   - **Reject** — Document reason. No work proceeds. Close the PCO.
   - **Revise** — Return to step 4 with specific revision requests.
6. **Execute change order.** All required parties sign. GC updates the contract sum, schedule, and drawings of record.
7. **Distribute CO.** Circulate the executed CO to: site superintendent, affected subcontractors, design team, procurement, and accounting.
8. **Close CO.** Mark as resolved in the change order log with execution date, final cost, and schedule adjustment.

## Decision Gates

| Gate | Condition to Pass | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| PCO submission | Complete description, reason, and preliminary estimate provided | SHB Inc. (GC/CM) |
| Pricing complete | All sub pricing received; design confirms scope; procurement confirms lead times | SHB Inc. (GC/CM) |
| CO approval | Owner's rep signs off on cost and schedule impact | SHB Group (Owner's Rep) |
| CO execution | All required signatures obtained | SHB Group (Owner's Rep); Principal sign-off required for COs at or above the project-defined approval threshold |

## CO Approval Threshold

COs at or above a project-defined approval threshold require SHB Group Principal sign-off in addition to Owner's Rep approval. The threshold is set per project at contract execution and documented in the project-specific configuration. COs below the threshold require Owner's Rep approval only.
