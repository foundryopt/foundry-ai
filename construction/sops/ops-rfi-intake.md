# RFI Intake

## Purpose

Ensure every Request for Information (RFI) from the field is logged, routed to the correct party, and resolved within a defined timeline — preventing construction delays caused by unanswered questions.

## Scope

All active construction projects across all entities. Applies to project managers, site superintendents, and design consultants.

## Control: No Work Without Written Response

**No construction work affected by an open RFI may proceed until a written response has been received, reviewed, and distributed.**

- Verbal responses do not constitute resolution.
- If field conditions require proceeding before a written response, the project manager must document the decision in writing, obtain owner's representative sign-off, and note the RFI as "proceeded at risk" in the log.
- Any cost or schedule impact from proceeding at risk is tracked against the RFI.

## Procedure

1. **Receive RFI.** Field team submits RFI with: subject, drawing/spec reference, location on site, and question detail.
2. **Log RFI.** Project manager assigns a sequential RFI number and records it in the project RFI log with submission date.
3. **Classify urgency.** Assign priority per the SLA table below.
4. **Route to responsible party.** Forward to architect, engineer, or consultant based on discipline. Copy the owner's representative.
5. **Track response.** Monitor against SLA. If the response window expires, escalate per the table below.
6. **Review response.** Project manager reviews the response for completeness. If incomplete, return to the responsible party with specific follow-up questions. The SLA clock resets on resubmission.
7. **Distribute resolution.** Circulate the answered RFI to all affected parties: site superintendent, subcontractors, and design team.
8. **Close RFI.** Mark as resolved in the RFI log with the resolution date and any drawing/spec revisions issued.

## SLA Table

| Priority | Definition | Routing Window | Response Window | Escalation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | Blocks active work on site | 4 hours | 48 hours | PM escalates to owner's rep and design principal |
| High | Impacts upcoming scheduled work (< 2 weeks) | 8 hours | 5 calendar days | PM escalates to owner's rep |
| Standard | Does not block current or near-term work | 24 hours | 7 calendar days | PM sends written reminder; escalates after 10 days |
| Low | Informational / close-out documentation | 48 hours | 14 calendar days | PM sends written reminder |
