# Property Management Onboarding

## Purpose

Ensure every completed project transitions into active property management with all required documentation, system access, and operational readiness confirmed — preventing gaps in service, warranty coverage, or tenant responsiveness.

## Scope

All projects reaching construction close-out or showroom-to-PM handoff. Applies to property management teams, the Development Concierge, and construction/showroom teams executing the handoff.

## Trigger

Property management onboarding begins when either:
- Construction issues a certificate of substantial completion (direct handoff), or
- The Development Concierge initiates a Showroom → PM handoff per `development/concierge/ops-project-continuity-handoff.md`.

## Control: No Occupancy Without Completed Onboarding

**No tenant or buyer may take occupancy until the property management onboarding checklist is complete and signed by the PM team lead.**

- If partial occupancy is required before full onboarding, the PM team lead must document the exception, list the incomplete items with deadlines, and obtain SHB Group (Owner's Rep) approval.

## Procedure

1. **Receive handoff package.** PM team receives the handoff package per the continuity handoff SOP. Confirm all items are present.
2. **Complete the onboarding checklist.** Verify each item in the checklist below.
3. **Set up property management systems.** Configure all operational systems per the system setup section.
4. **Assign on-site staff.** Confirm staffing plan, roles, and emergency contact chain.
5. **Conduct building walkthrough.** PM team lead walks the property with the GC superintendent to verify condition, note any punch-list items, and confirm building systems are operational.
6. **Accept or escalate.** If all checklist items pass, PM team lead signs the acceptance. If items are incomplete, document gaps and assign SLAs per the handoff SOP.
7. **Begin operations.** Property is active in the PM system. Tenant/buyer communications are live.
