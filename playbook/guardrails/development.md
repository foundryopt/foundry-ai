# Development Guardrails

## Deal Selection

### Never fall in love with the building
**The rule:** Underwrite the dirt, not the design. If the numbers don't work with a boring box, 
the deal doesn't work. Beautiful design is how we *add* value — it's never the reason to *do* a deal.

### Rezone risk = timeline risk
**The rule:** Budget 6–12 months for any project requiring a zoning change. 
Aldermanic support is not a guarantee — get it in writing (letter of support) before going under contract. 
Always have an as-of-right fallback plan that still pencils.

### Don't chase neighborhoods
**The rule:** Stick to neighborhoods where we have completed projects and comp data. 
Expanding to a new market doubles risk — new subs, new inspectors, unknown buyer/renter profile. 
New market = new playbook. One at a time.

### Seller motivation matters more than price
**The rule:** A motivated seller at fair price closes faster than a cheap deal with a difficult seller. 
Time is carry cost. 60 extra days negotiating a $30K discount on a $2M deal costs more than $30K in interest and opportunity.
