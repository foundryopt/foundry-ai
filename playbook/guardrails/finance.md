# Finance Guardrails

## Pro Forma

### Contingency is not optional
**The rule:** Minimum 5% hard cost contingency on as-of-right projects. 
10% on any project with a rezone, unusual site, or first-time building type. 
Anyone who says "we won't need contingency" has never built anything.

### Exit values should make you nervous
**The rule:** Underwrite exit values at 90% of current comps. If the deal only works 
at top-of-market pricing, it doesn't work. The market will correct at the worst possible time.

### Carry cost is the silent killer
**The rule:** Every month of delay costs real money — interest, taxes, insurance, 
opportunity cost. Self-cert speed exists for a reason. Track carry cost per project per month 
and treat schedule slippage as a financial metric, not just a construction one.

## Cash Flow

### Draw schedules must match build sequence
**The rule:** Never front-load draws to cover other projects. When draws get ahead of work, 
you lose leverage with the lender and create a cash crunch at closeout when retainage hits.

### Don't commingle project funds
**The rule:** Every project should have its own clear cash accounting. 
Robbing Peter to pay Paul across projects is how companies go under.

### The gap between "sold" and "funded" will hurt you
**The rule:** Investor soft commits are not cash. LP capital calls can take 30–60 days. 
Bridge financing or personal guarantees may be needed to cover the gap. 
Plan for it.
