# Procurement Guardrails

## Lead Times

### If it's not ordered 12 weeks out, it's already late
**The rule:** Windows, cabinets, appliances, elevators — anything custom or imported needs 
12–16 week lead time minimum. Order at DD, not at rough-in. 
The number one schedule killer in SHB history is late material.

### Don't spec what you can't re-order
**The rule:** If a material is discontinued, single-source, or special-order with 
no substitute, it's a risk. Always confirm availability and identify a backup spec 
before it goes in the finish database.

### Verify dimensions before ordering
**The rule:** Measure twice, order once. Field dimensions vs. drawing dimensions 
cause the most returns and re-orders. Site-verify openings before ordering windows, 
doors, and custom millwork.

## Vendors

### Get it in writing — every time
**The rule:** Verbal quotes are worthless. Every price, every lead time, every spec 
must be in a written quote or PO. When a vendor says "about 8 weeks," 
the PO says the exact delivery date and penalty terms.

### The showroom sample is not the production run
**The rule:** Always request a production sample or batch reference before final order. 
Color, texture, and finish can vary between showroom display and what shows up on site.
