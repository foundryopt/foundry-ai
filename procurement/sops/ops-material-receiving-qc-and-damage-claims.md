# Material Receiving, QC, and Damage Claims

## Purpose

Ensure every material delivery is inspected on arrival, documented with photo evidence, and accepted or rejected per quality standards — preventing installation of damaged or non-conforming materials and preserving claim rights against carriers and vendors.

## Scope

All active construction projects across all entities. Applies to site superintendents, procurement coordinators, and subcontractor foremen receiving materials on site.

## Control: No Storage or Installation Without QC Sign-Off

**No delivered material may be moved to storage or installed until the receiving QC checklist is completed and signed by the site superintendent or designated receiving agent.**

- Materials failing QC are segregated immediately and tagged "rejected — do not use."
- Damage claims must be initiated within 48 hours of delivery to preserve carrier/vendor liability.

## Procedure

### Receiving

1. **Verify delivery against PO.** Compare the packing slip / bill of lading to the purchase order. Confirm item descriptions, quantities, and specifications match.
2. **Perform visual inspection.** Inspect all items for visible damage, defects, or non-conformance using the QC checklist below.
3. **Document with photos.** Photograph each delivery: overview of shipment, close-ups of labels/markings, and close-ups of any damage or defects. Timestamp all photos.
4. **Complete QC checklist.** Fill in the checklist for every line item. Sign and date.
5. **Accept or reject.**
   - **Accept** — Material passes QC. Proceed to storage.
   - **Accept with exceptions** — Minor issues noted; material usable with conditions documented.
   - **Reject** — Material fails QC. Segregate and tag. Initiate damage/deficiency claim.

### Storage

6. **Store per manufacturer requirements.** Follow manufacturer storage instructions (covered, climate-controlled, elevated, etc.).
7. **Label and log.** Tag stored materials with project name, PO number, and submittal number. Record storage location in the material log.
8. **Protect from damage.** Ensure stored materials are protected from weather, traffic, and adjacent trade activity.

### Damage Claims

9. **Notify vendor/carrier within 48 hours.** Send written notice with: PO number, delivery date, description of damage, and photo evidence.
10. **Log the claim.** Record the claim in the project material log with claim date, vendor/carrier, and status.
11. **Coordinate replacement.** Builiq Inc. (Procurement) coordinates replacement delivery and updates the lead-time tracker.
12. **Track claim resolution.** Monitor until credit, replacement, or settlement is received. Close the claim with resolution details.

## Photo Evidence Requirements

| When | What to Photograph | Minimum Photos |
|---|---|---|
| Every delivery | Overview of truck/shipment as received | 1 |
| Every delivery | Packing slip / bill of lading | 1 |
| Every delivery | Labels and markings on each distinct item | 1 per item type |
| Damage found | Close-up of each damaged area | 2+ per area |
| Rejection | Segregated material with "rejected" tag visible | 1 |
